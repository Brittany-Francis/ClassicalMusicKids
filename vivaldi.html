Antonio Vivaldi

Country of Origin: Venice, Italy

Years of Life: March 1678 - July 1741

Musical Era: Baroque

About: Vivaldi was born in Venice, Italy in 1678. His father, originally a barber, became a violinist and taught his son to play too! They traveled across Italy playing together. Vivaldi's first occupation was actually a priest! They nicknamed him "The Red Priest" due to his red hair. Unfortunately due to some health issues, he was unable to continue this profession, and switched to composing music. He wrote many concertos, sacred choral works, and operas. He is most well known for his concerto "The Four Seasons". Each movement represents a different season; Spring, Summer, Autumn, and Winter.
