Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky

Country of Origin: Russia

Years of Life: May 1840 - November 1893

Musical Era: Romantic

About: Born in Kamsko-Votkinsk, Russia in 1840, Tchaikovsky started learning piano at age 5. His family moved around a lot, and his mother eventually passed away, leaving Tchaikovsky to cope through playing music. He was pushed to study law by his father. Eventually he realized his true passion was music and enrolled in the St. Petersburg Conservatory, where he learned flute, organ, and composition. His compositions had a distinctly Russian style. Tchaikovsky wrote many opera's, ballet's, and orchestral compositions. His most famous works include his 3 ballet's, "Swan Lake", "Sleeping Beauty", and "The Nutcracker". He died from cholera in 1893.

Fun Facts:

  • He gave up on studying law and decided to study music at the St. Petersburg Conservatory.
  • He had a patroness named Nadezhda von Meck, who provided him with money to continue composing.
  • He travelled to the U.S. for the opening of Carnegie Hall.
