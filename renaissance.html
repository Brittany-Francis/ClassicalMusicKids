About The Renaissance Era

The Renaissance Era of music occurred between the years 1400-1600. During this time, music became more tonal, as opposed to the modal music of the medieval era that preceded it. Polyphonic music, or music where multiple parts can occur simultaneously, originated during this time period and continued to advance in the following eras. Some of the major composers of this time include William Byrd, Thomas Tallis, Giovanni Pierluigi da Palestrina, Orlando di Lasso, Guillaume DuFay, and Claudio Monteverdi. The Renaissance was a period of exploration and new discoveries, and with this came new instruments. Some of the instruments used during this time period include the viol, lyre, lute, harpsichord, harp, hurdy-gury, tambourine, reed pipe, and recorder. New musical styles emerged, such as masses, madrigals, motets, and chansons.
