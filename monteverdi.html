Claudio Monteverdi

Country of Origin: Italy

Years of Life: May 1567 - November 1643

Musical Era: Renaissance

About: Monteverdi was born in Cremona, Italy in 1567. He was a court musician in Mantua for many years, playing vivuola, which could mean either the viola da gamba or viola da braccio. Monteverdi was a composer of both the Renaissance and Baroque eras, utilizing the polyphonic compositional methods of the Renaissance era, and the "basso continuo", which was a technique that can be linked to the Baroque era. He wrote many motets, madrigals, and operas. He passed away in Venice, Italy at the age of 76.

Fun Facts:

  • His full name is Claudio Giovanni Antonio Monteverdi.
  • He composed both secular and scared music.
  • His opera L'Orfeo is still performed today.
