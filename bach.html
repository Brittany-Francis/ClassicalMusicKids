Johann Sebastian Bach

Country of Origin: Eisenach, Germany

Years of Life: 1685-1750

Musical Era: Baroque

About: Johann Sebastian Bach was born to a family of musicians in 1685. He played organ, harpsichord, viola, and violin, and wrote a lot of music for these instruments. Bach worked in the church for part of his life, composing many religious pieces of music, such as masses. He also composed a lot of other types of works, including concertos, sonatas, cantatas, suites, and works for keyboard. Some of his most famous pieces include "Toccata and Fugue in D Minor", "The Brandenburg Concertos", and the "Goldberg Variations".

Fun Facts:

  • He wrote over 1000 pieces of music.
  • Bach had 20 children.
  • Bach once was thrown in jail by a Duke for trying to quit a job he didn't like.
