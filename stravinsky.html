Igor Stravinsky

Country of Origin: Russia

Years of Life: June 1882 - April 1971

Musical Era: 20th Century/Modern

About: Born in St. Petersburg, Russia, Stravinsky was the son of an opera singer, learning to love music at an early age. He learned piano starting at age 9, and eventually went on to study composition with another famous Russian composer, Nikolai Rimsky-Korsakov. Stravinsky is perhaps most well known for his 3 ballets, "The Firebird", "Petrushka", and "The Rite of Spring". However he was also a pivotal composer between the 20th century and modern styles of music. His earlier works employ the use of the neoclassical techniques, while some of his later works include the use of the 12-tone technique. He died on April 6 at the age of 88 from heart failure.

Fun Facts:

  • Stravinsky originally studied law, but decided he would rather study music.
  • During the opening night of his ballet "Rite of Spring", the audience was shocked by the music and began to riot.
  • He had citizenship in Russia, then France, then the United States.
