Ludwig van Beethoven

Country of Origin: Bonn, Germany

Years of Life: December 1770-March 1827

Musical Era: Classical-Romantic

About: Born in Bonn, Germany in 1770, Beethoven was first introduced to music at an early age. His father, a musician, taught him to play the keyboard, but he also had the privilege to learn violin and viola from other local musicians. He was a great performer and composer, writing many works that we still hear in the movies, commercials, and tv today. Some of his most famous works include his 5th and 9th symphonies, which have melodies that are still highly recognizable. Aside from 9 symphonies, he also wrote sonatas for piano and violin, string quartets, and even 1 opera called "Fidelio" .

Fun Facts:

  • Beethoven was a part of both the classical and romantic eras of music.
  • He starting losing his hearing at age 26.
  • His father taught him his first piano lessons.
