About The Baroque Era

From the years of 1600-1750, baroque music flourished. The music was highly complex and ornamented, especially compared with music of the Renaissance. Baroque music was prevalent in Europe, being practiced in many countries including Italy, Germany, France, and England. Some of the most famous baroque composers include Johann Sebastian Bach, Antonio Vivaldi, George Frideric Handel, Henry Purcell, and Jean-Baptiste Lully. These composers composed many types of works, including operas, oratorios, cantatas, sonatas, suites, and concerti.
