About The Classical Era

The classical era of music began in 1750, after the death of Johann Sebastian Bach, and continued until 1830. Some of the most famous composers of this time were Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, Franz Joseph Haydn, Ludwig van Beethoven, and Franz Schubert. Music was being written in the classical style all over Europe, in countries such as Germany, Austria, and Italy. The harpsichord was replaced by the pianoforte, which is an earlier version of what we now call the piano. Orchestras now had a woodwinds section, including instruments such as the flute, oboe,clarinet, and bassoon. Music became more homophonic, meaning it had one melody with harmonic accompaniment, rather than multiple different melodies being played simultaneously. Some of the popular forms of music written during this era include symphonies, concertos, sonatas, and string quartets.
