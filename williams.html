John Williams

Country of Origin: United States

Years of Life: February 1932 - Present

Musical Era: Modern

About: Born in New York, John Williams learned music at an early age. His father was a jazz drummer and percussionist and insisted his children learn music. Williams even formed a band with his friends and siblings in his youth! He joined the Army Band in 1951, playing piano, arranging and conducting for them. Afterwards, he headed to Julliard to study music. He became interested in film music and ended up becoming one of the most prolific composers of film scores. His big break was the composition for the movie "Jaws", on which he worked with director Steven Spielberg. They have worked on many films together since. Some of his most famous works include the themes to "Harry Potter", "Indiana Jones", and "Jurassic Park".

Fun Facts:

  • John Williams does not use any music software to write his music and prefers to compose using pencil and paper.
  • He has won 5 oscars for his film scores and was nominated 54 times!
  • He learned to play the piano, cello, trumpet and trombone in his youth.
